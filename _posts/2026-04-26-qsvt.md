description: "Quantum Singular Value Transformation (QSVT) is a quantum algorithmic framework for applying polynomial transformations to the singular values of block-encoded matrices. This tutorial motivates QSVT by showing how quantum signal processing (QSP) extends from scalar signal transformations to singular-value transformations of block-encoded matrices. It explains admissible polynomials, phase angles, block-encodings, and projector-controlled phase operators, then implements examples in PennyLane. It then uses QSVT to approximate matrix inversion, visualizes the resulting circuit, and discusses its role as a unifying framework for quantum algorithms."
