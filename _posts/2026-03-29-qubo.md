description: "A walkthrough of Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization (QUBO), a mathematical framework for expressing combinatorial optimization problems as the minimization of a quadratic objective function over binary variables. The tutorial defines the QUBO problem statement and its canonical form, examines how constraints and penalty terms are incorporated into the unconstrained formulation, and presents techniques for encoding a variety of combinatorial problems into QUBO form. It situates QUBO within the broader hierarchy of optimization problem classes and explores its equivalence with the Ising model, a connection that underlies its relevance to quantum solvers.  A QUBO formulation is then implemented using the D-Wave Ocean SDK, before the tutorial briefly introduces minor embedding as the mechanism by which QUBO problems are mapped onto the quantum annealing hardware."
