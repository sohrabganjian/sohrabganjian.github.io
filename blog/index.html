  • Perspectives on Quantum Computing: Selected Algorithms and the Evolving Quantum Computing Landscape

    A high-level perspective on quantum computing that reviews selected quantum algorithms, their connections to other algorithms, and common algorithmic techniques. The discussion then examines the evolving quantum software ecosystem, the current computing landscape, hardware platforms, commercial cloud access, pricing models, and what the available scientific evidence does and does not establish. It concludes by reflecting on how far the field has come, where it currently stands, and the directions in which it may be moving.

  • Shor's Algorithm

    Shor's algorithm is a quantum algorithm for integer factorization, first presented by Peter Shor in 1994. It demonstrates that a quantum computer can theoretically factor integers in polynomial time, giving an exponential speedup over any known classical factoring algorithm for a cryptographically relevant problem with real-world utility. Shor's algorithm is one of the most fundamental quantum algorithms. This tutorial explains Shor's algorithm, gives a heuristic analysis of its probability of success as a probabilistic quantum algorithm, discusses gate complexity, resource estimates, hardware demonstrations of quantum factoring, and implications for classical and quantum complexity theory. It then implements a small example of Shor's algorithm in Qiskit by factoring 15, with background material on number theory and the historical development of the algorithm.

  • QSVT

    Quantum Singular Value Transformation (QSVT) is a quantum algorithmic framework for applying polynomial transformations to the singular values of block-encoded matrices. This tutorial motivates QSVT by showing how quantum signal processing (QSP) extends from scalar signal transformations to singular-value transformations of block-encoded matrices. It explains admissible polynomials, phase angles, block-encodings, and projector-controlled phase operators, then implements examples in PennyLane. It then uses QSVT to approximate matrix inversion, visualizes the resulting circuit, and discusses its role as a unifying framework for quantum algorithms.

  • QAOA

    The Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm (QAOA) is a variational gate-based method for combinatorial optimization on quantum gate-based hardware. This tutorial derives QAOA from adiabatic evolution, explains the cost and mixer Hamiltonians, and maps a QUBO to a cost Hamiltonian. It then applies QAOA to a number partitioning instance, building the circuit in PennyLane, optimizing parameters with a classical optimizer, and interpreting the output distribution against uniform and near-optimal baselines.

  • QUBO

    A walkthrough of Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization (QUBO), a mathematical framework for expressing combinatorial optimization problems as the minimization of a quadratic objective function over binary variables. The tutorial defines the QUBO problem statement and its canonical form, examines how constraints and penalty terms are incorporated into the unconstrained formulation, and presents techniques for encoding a variety of combinatorial problems into QUBO form. It situates QUBO within the broader hierarchy of optimization problem classes and explores its equivalence with the Ising model, a connection that underlies its relevance to quantum solvers. A QUBO formulation is then implemented using the D-Wave Ocean SDK, before the tutorial briefly introduces minor embedding as the mechanism by which QUBO problems are mapped onto the quantum annealing hardware.
